## Rawson and Tupper: theory/practice bridge

Source: ScienceDirect, Basic Ship Theory
URL: https://www.sciencedirect.com/book/monograph/9780750653985/basic-ship-theory
Reliability: publisher page.

Key extraction:
- Basic Ship Theory is a standard introductory text for naval architecture students and experienced designers.
- Volume 1 covers geometry, measurement, safety, structural strength, flotation, trim, and stability.
- Volume 2 covers dynamics, hydrodynamics, manoeuvrability, seakeeping, case studies, and maritime design.
- It emphasizes considering the environment in design.

Distilled principle:
- A ship is a coupled system: geometry, displacement, stability, structure, hydrodynamics, propulsion, and mission interact.

## Evans design spiral and later criticism

Source: MDPI, Systems Engineering for Naval Ship Design Evolution
URL: https://www.mdpi.com/2077-1312/12/2/210
Reliability: peer-reviewed open access article.

Key extraction:
- The ship design spiral was originally introduced by J.H. Evans in 1959.
- Watson published design spirals for merchant ships and warships in 1998.
- Later authors criticized the spiral as a weak representation of concept design, especially for complex naval vessels where decisions rely on experience, design lanes, rules of thumb, preferences, and imagination.

Distilled principle:
- Iteration is necessary but not enough. Complex ship design requires systems engineering, requirement traceability, interface management, and risk-based trade studies.

Use in final skill:
- Add "spiral is a reminder, not a method" warning.

## IMO GHG Strategy: decarbonization as design constraint

Source: IMO, "IMO's work to cut GHG emissions from ships"
URL: https://www.imo.org/en/mediacentre/hottopics/pages/cutting-ghg-emissions.aspx
Reliability: official IMO page.

Key extraction:
- IMO adopted the 2023 IMO GHG Strategy at MEPC 80.
- Target: net-zero GHG emissions from international shipping by or around 2050.
- Indicative checkpoints: reduce total annual GHG emissions by at least 20%, striving for 30%, by 2030; at least 70%, striving for 80%, by 2040, compared to 2008.
- Carbon intensity of international shipping is to reduce by at least 40% by 2030 compared to 2008.
- Zero or near-zero GHG technologies, fuels, or energy sources should represent at least 5%, striving for 10%, of energy used by international shipping by 2030.

Distilled principle:
- Modern ship design must treat carbon intensity, fuel pathway, operational profile, and retrofit potential as core requirements, not afterthoughts.

## IACS and IMO as living constraints

Sources:
- IACS CSR: https://iacs.org.uk/resolutions/common-structural-rules
- IMO IS Code: https://imo-epublications.org/content/books/9789280117202
- IMO GHG: https://www.imo.org/en/mediacentre/hottopics/pages/cutting-ghg-emissions.aspx

Distilled principle:
- Engineering judgment operates inside a changing regulatory envelope.
- A "good design" that is non-compliant or hard to certify is not a good ship design.
