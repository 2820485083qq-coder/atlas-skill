# 03-stability-structures-loads

Purpose: collect stability, sea-load, structural design, and rule-basis sources.

## IMO Intact Stability Code 2008

Source: IMO e-Publications, International Code on Intact Stability 2008, 2020 Edition
URL: https://imo-epublications.org/content/books/9789280117202
Reliability: official IMO publication page.

Key extraction:
- The 2008 IS Code presents mandatory and recommendatory stability criteria and other measures for safe operation of ships.
- The stated purpose is to minimize risk to ships, personnel, and environment.
- 2020 Edition includes amendments entering into force by January 1, 2020, explanatory notes, and unified interpretations.

Distilled principle:
- Stability is not just GM. It is a criteria set involving righting ability, operating conditions, dynamic behavior, and documentation.

Use in final skill:
- Add stability gate: loading conditions, KG/GM assumptions, GZ curve, free-surface effects, weather/wave criteria, damage/intact applicability.

## SNAME PNA: strength of ships and ocean structures

Source: SNAME, Principles of Naval Architecture, Strength of Ships and Ocean Structures
URL: https://sname.org/principles-naval-architecture
Reliability: official professional society source.

Key extraction:
- The strength volume covers dynamic sea-load predictions, nonlinear load effects, slamming and impact, collisions and grounding.
- It covers long-term extreme and cumulative fatigue effects, finite element methods, ultimate strength of hull girder and components, structural safety and reliability concepts.
- It notes special ship types and sizes may require first-principles design when history is limited.

Distilled principle:
- Structure review must follow load path and failure mode, not only rule scantling.
- Rules are minimum compliance; first-principles analysis is needed when novelty or risk is high.

## Hughes and Paik: rational ship structural design

Source: SNAME, Ship Structural Analysis and Design
URL: https://sname.org/ship-structural-analysis-and-design
Reliability: official SNAME book page.

Key extraction:
- Hughes and Paik focus on preliminary structural design, where principal structural member sizing has the greatest design freedom.
- The approach is rationally based and suitable for preliminary structural design.
- The method allows optimization by least life-cycle cost, least weight, or combinations.
- Underlying theory has been verified through extensive testing and implemented in MAESTRO.

Distilled principle:
- Structural design is an optimization under constraints, not a single stress check.
- Choose the measure of merit explicitly: weight, life-cycle cost, fatigue life, repairability, production simplicity.

## IACS Common Structural Rules

Source: IACS Common Structural Rules for Bulk Carriers and Oil Tankers
URL: https://iacs.org.uk/resolutions/common-structural-rules
Reliability: official IACS source.

Key extraction:
- CSR govern construction and maintenance of bulk carriers and oil tankers.
- They aim to ensure safety and structural integrity.
- They are maintained to reflect advances in data, technologies, materials, and methodologies.
- A single harmonized CSR for bulk carriers and oil tankers now exists.

Distilled principle:
- Classification rules are living technical standards, not fixed textbook formulas.
- Always check the latest applicable rule edition and vessel type applicability.

## Faltinsen: sea loads and offshore structures

Source: National Library of Australia catalogue / Google Books records for Sea Loads on Ships and Offshore Structures
URLs:
- https://catalogue.nla.gov.au/catalog/1897278
- https://books.google.com/books/about/Sea_Loads_on_Ships_and_Offshore_Structur.html?id=qZq4Rs2DZXoC
Reliability: catalogue and book metadata; use the actual book for technical details.

Key extraction:
- The book covers wind, wave, and current loads on ships and offshore structures and applications to real structures.
- Topic terms include added mass, damping, drift forces, slamming, strip theory, Morison equation, wave loads, resonance, and relative motion.

Distilled principle:
- Calm-water performance is only one layer. Sea loads, resonance, slamming, fatigue, and operations define whether a ship survives and works.
